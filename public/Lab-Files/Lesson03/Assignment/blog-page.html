October 31st, 2016

This is Halloween, Halloween, Halloween... Happy Halloween everybody!!
Also, a big Happy Birthday to my grandmother in-law!
Like most Halloweens of my adult life, I was not thinking that I would dress up this year.
However, I found my husband's red bowtie and my inner child yearned to come out.
So in the only work appropriate costume I could think of, I went as the Doctor. The 11th Doctor
to be exact, since bowties and fezzes are cool. I also carried around a purse that looks like
Mary Poppin's bag with an umbrella and tape measure in it just in case no one went for the Doctor idea.
I was pleasantly surprised that more peopled guessed my costume as the Doctor than as Mary Poppins.
But that doesn't mean that I am not still 'practically perfect in every way'.

October 15th, 2016

For those of you who aren't aware and are too lazy to read the About Me section (no worries, I feel ya), I am in a band
called: Erin & Evan. Named after yours truly and the amazingly talented, Evan. Super easy for us to remember.
Well, we had a show today at Alamo Beer Company, it was a three hour set from 2-5pm. Now these sets are usually pretty
grueling since even though it's 'October', it's still summer weather here in Texas. But luckily, we had a nice breeze today
which meant that people could sit out on the patio to listen to us without sweating their coconuts off. We are working on
getting our album finished, it is moving slowly, but stea...nope just slowly, but eventually we will get there to a
finished album/cd/mp3 etc. You can find the link to some of our older songs in the SoundCloud link in the Music section of this blog.

October 11th, 2016

Today is a very special day! Not only is it the first blog entry on my "The Musical Nerd" blog (aka You are Here),
but it is also the first day of my coding classes. I am extremely excited to finally get started and honestly
(here comes the earning of the title 'Nerd'), I'm excited to go back to school! Well not school school,
but to be in a classroom setting again and learning something new... makes me giddy. Now those of you who read the
'About Me' section are probably asking, "But you are a musician, your passion is music, why aren't you going back to
school for music?" Well... short answer: because music doesn't pay my student loans. I would rather have a job that
I enjoy that gives me the paycheck to be able to continue to pursue my passion and dreams than stay at my hourly
'music' job, where I don't actually do any music and get grossly underpaid. So yeah... I have a feeling Code Monkey is going
to be stuck in my head for the majority of this class. Oh and a Happy Happy Birthday tomorrow to my sister in-law,
Alison, who is the writer of my favorite transplant Texan in Seatle blog, Swears and Sweet Tea.

About Me aka The Musical Nerd

Hello, my name is Erin and I am your run-of-the-mill musically talented nerd. Texas born and raised
on a farm is where I did not spend most of my days. Try more like suburbs of Houston. Went to college up
in almost Oklahoma North Texas at Austin College (Go Roos!) and against all judgement studies music.
Now don't get me wrong, I loved getting my degree in Music, but I do wish I had double majored in something
relatively marketable. And by marketable, I mean for more than $9/hr that wasn't a call center. Hindsight is
20/20. Anyways...met my husband at college, got married a week after graduation (don't try this at home kids),
and promptly moved away to New Hampshire. All the while, I am a big BIG fan of shows like Doctor Who, Sherlock,
and Agents of Shield, and have become ingrained in the fandom life in my spare time (aka 'Nerd' part of me). I am
half of a folk duo band called 'Erin & Evan' where Evan and I sing, play guitar (Evan), play mandolin (me), and
make/write generally good music. We are working on an album of originals that we have written and recorded with the
help of our good friend, Reo. I am positive you will hear all about the progress of this in the above blog posts.
Enough about me, now go read things that I thought of and wrote. DFTBA!
